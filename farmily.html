RU Hacks - Hackathon

Farmily

Connect farmers with excess produce to local consumers to reduce food waste due to COVID-19

Devpost | Github

Problem

I want to reduce the amount of COVID-19 food waste by redistributing surplus to those who need it.

Solution

Allow people to purchase food directly from local farms with extra product through an online marketplace.

My Role:
Database management, Backend development, UI/UX Design

Tools used:
Javascript, HTML5, CSS3, Firebase, Google Maps API, Node.js

Team:
Amy Han and Keke Zhang

Duration:
36 hours

During COVID-19 dairy farmers alone are dumping as much as 3.7 million gallons of milk each day.

- Statistic from Dairy Farmers of America

What it does

Family enables farmers to sign up and list their excess produce on an easy to access online platform. Afterwards anyone nearby can search for farms and produce nearby and purchase food that otherwise would have been thrown out. This allows for a simple farm to table solution eliminating the middlemen in the strained supply chains.

What I did

I designed the layout of the website through Figma and utilized UI/UX design principles to create an intuitive and easy to understand layout while maintaining functionality. I also converted the Figma design into HTML and CSS so that it could be hosted on Github Pages.

I also worked on the backend and used Javascript to link the frontend to a Google Firebase database. This allowed us to store important data such as user locations, farm inventory, and produce prices.

Challenges & acheivements

This project was a large challenge for me as it was the first time I had used HTML, CSS, Figma, Javascript, or any of the API’s before. Everything I used was new to me which was an uphill struggle especially in the short time span. This meant much of this project was geared towards learning and the development and improvement of technical and soft skills for me.

However, in the end I was able to quickly grasp the concepts and apply my knowledge well as we were able to achieve our goal within the tight timelines. Going from being unable to understand the documentation to creating a working solution to a worldwide issue was a magical moment to me as I saw my effort materialize into something that could provide value to others.

My takeaways

The most important thing that I was able to learn from this project was the importance of understanding the fundamentals. I found myself tripping over errors in my code frequently in the beginning. However, after a while I took the time to review the concepts from the ground up so that I could understand how to apply my knowledge better. In the end this allowed me to quickly grasp the basics of HTML, CSS, Javascript, product design, and API’s quickly and efficiently.

In terms of soft skills this project let me appreciate the value of working with others more as I was able to learn so much more by having others assist me through the learning process. Through effective communication and teamwork we were able to pull off a feat that seemed impossible to us just a few hours earlier.
