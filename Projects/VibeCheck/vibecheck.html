Hack the Northeast - Hackathon

Vibe Check

Find the mood of your Spotify playlist and filter out songs that don’t match the mood

Website | Devpost | Github

Problem

I want to keep the vibe going when I’m listening to music and don’t want songs which breaks my mood.

Solution

Allow people to identify the genre or “vibe” of their playlists and let them easily remove songs that could break the mood.

My Role:
Implementing machine learning, Backend development, UI/UX Design

Tools used:
Javascript, HTML5, CSS3, Brain.js, Heroku, Node.js, Spotify Web API, Illustrator, Figma

Team:
Amy Han and Andrew Yu

Duration:
36 hours

It takes 23 minutes and 15 seconds to refocus after an interruption.

- Researchers from the University of California

What it does

Vibe Check connects you to your Spotify playlists and identifies its genre. We’ll surface songs from the playlist which don’t match the genre to blacklist, you can drag the slider to adjust how strict you want the song filter to be. Once satisfied, we’ll automatically create a newly filtered playlist in your Spotify.

What I did

I created and trained the neural network to classify the genre of the playlists, by creating a dataset of over 1000+ songs with varying genres. I trained several models this way with varying hidden layers and learning rates and ran them on another dataset to pick the most accurate model. The end result was a model that could classify a single song with high accuracy.

Challenges & acheivements

This project was certainly very challenging as the aim of the project was not only to realize our concept but to also learn and develop new skills too. In the tight time span of 36 hours I had massive improvements on my understanding of neural networks as I had to learn more about forwards and backwards propagation, XOR gates, hidden layers, etc.

By the end of the hackathon I accomplished my goals of learning new concepts and implementing them into a function program! I had stepped outside of my comfort zone by using tools I had never touched before such as node.js and brain.js and also using AJAX calls. Being able to cohesively use all these new tools to create a usable application strengthened my learning and collaboration skills.

My product design skills were also improved as I worked to use new concepts in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to implement my Figma designs. As the creator of almost all the frontend I needed to check my product understanding, time management and communication to create the user interface while also working on various other roles in the backend at the same time!

My takeaways

To me this project certainly was not a cakewalk. Thank you to my amazing team for all the work that they did, without them this project would not have been possible. I truly enjoyed working together with them so that we could build off of each other’s work and learn so much more than if I did this solo. Team work really did make the dream work!

Personally, I had to juggle several roles at the same time while learning new concepts and immediately applying them making me a much more flexible individual. There were times of frustration and times of happiness as the project moved forward. I was excited to learn so much and it really opened my eyes to what code can do. The satisfaction of running my neural network and having it predict the song after hours of work through the night was incredibly reliving.

Finally, I’m glad that my work was able to make a difference to others. After hearing that others used my program and enjoyed it’s use it really made me realize that much of my joy in creating projects not only comes from satisfying my desire to learn but to also see that I could benefit others with my work!
